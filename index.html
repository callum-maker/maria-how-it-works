Maria — How It Works

A visual map of what the companion can do, how information flows through it, and — importantly — who influences what information. Each tablet is one resident's private device; everything below lives on that device unless noted.

1What she can do

Capabilities, grouped. Each is either a live conversation skill, a proactive behaviour, or a staff tool.

💬 Natural conversation

Warm, short spoken replies via Claude, tuned for older adults; remembers the person across turns and days.

📖 Life-story reminiscence

Gently walks through life chapters, captures memories, comforts on grief, exits on distress.

🗓️ "What's on today"

Daily routine + schedule (upcoming only), then invites other activities.

🎛️ Tools

Weather, positive news, jokes, quotes, Wikipedia facts, song lyrics, poems, scripture, "on this day", web search.

📻 Music & radio

Plays internet radio / old songs; tap to stop.

📷 Family photos

Shows & talks about photos; weaves a new photo into the chat right away and asks if they'd like to see it.

💊 Medication reminders

Chime + on-screen card + gentle spoken line, at set times (time-critical, can interrupt).

🔁 Proactive routines

Scheduled trivia / news / music / check-ins / life-story chats — delivered only on a natural lull.

🛡️ Guardrails

No medical advice, crisis/emergency detection, avoid-topics honoured (incl. filtering the news) — with an optional "allow if the resident asks" override.

😴 Reads the room

"I'm resting / don't want to talk / good night" → goes quiet; offers rest on subtle cues.

📊 Staff insight

Wellbeing notes, conversation history, "what I've learned", life-story book (PDF).

🚀 Remote updates

New logic/prompts pushed over-the-air; auto-applies on idle. No cable.

Key idea: staff/family set the stage (who the person is, what to raise, what to avoid, the day's plan). The resident fills it in just by talking — Maria listens and writes memories, life-story entries, and wellbeing signals. Everything staff-set + Maria-learned is re-assembled into her prompt every single turn, so she always speaks with the latest context. External look-ups only add transient facts to a reply and are never stored as "who they are."

8Cutting cost as we scale

Today's ~$1.50 / active-hour (§7) is a list-price, single-device figure — and ~88% of it is the Azure voice, which is exactly where the biggest levers are. Here's the path to materially lower unit cost as we go from one tablet to a fleet. (The AI "brain" is already the cheapest tier at ~10% of cost — not a target.)

Tools the brain can call (15)

ToolDescription
web_searchGeneral internet search — use ONLY when no more specific tool fits, for current or real-time information (recent events, prices, local details, anything you're unsure of).
get_weatherGet the current weather and today's forecast for a place. Use when the person asks about the weather.
on_this_dayGet notable historical events that happened on today's date — lovely for sparking memories and conversation.
tell_jokeGet a short, clean, family-friendly joke. Use when the person could use a laugh.
get_quoteGet an inspiring or thoughtful quote to share.
look_up_topicLook up a short, reliable summary about a person, place, event, or thing (Wikipedia). Use for factual questions like 'tell me about Winston Churchill' or 'what is the Eiffel Tower'.
get_lyricsGet the words to a song for a sing-along or to reminisce. Use when the person mentions a song or wants to sing.
get_poemGet a classic public-domain poem to read aloud (older poets — Wordsworth, Kipling, Dickinson, Tennyson, etc.). Optionally by title or author; otherwise a random one. NOTE: it does NOT have 20th-century poets like Robert Frost, Maya Angelou, etc. — if they ask for one of those, or a specific poem you already know well, just recite it warmly from your own memory instead of using this tool.
get_scriptureGet a Bible passage or comforting verse to share. Optionally a reference like 'Psalm 23' or 'John 3:16'; otherwise a comforting verse. For residents who find comfort in scripture.
get_newsGet the top world news headlines. Use when the person asks what's happening in the news or about current events.
play_radioPlay internet radio for the person — music OR news/talk radio. Use when they ask to hear music (a song style, era, or artist: 'put on some Frank Sinatra', 'play swing music', 'some classical') OR to hear the news / talk radio ('put on the news', 'is there a news station', 'some talk radio'). Pass a genre/category when you can ('news', 'talk', 'crooners', 'big band', 'classical', '1950s', 'country gospel') — those find stations more reliably than a specific artist or program. If it returns not_found, call again with a broader category. While it plays, the screen shows a music view and the person taps it to stop — tell them so.
stop_radioStop the radio music that's currently playing. Use when the person asks to turn it off or stop the music.
show_family_photoShow one of the person's family photos on the screen and talk about it warmly. Use when they ask to see a photo, or when talking about family/loved ones and a photo would bring them joy (especially while reminiscing). Pass a short description of which one, matching the list of available photos. Only call this if photos are actually available. After showing it, describe it warmly using what you know about who and what is in it.
close_family_photoPut away / close the family photo currently on the screen. Use when the person asks to close it or put it away, when they're done looking at it, or when the conversation moves on to something else.
rest_quietlyGo quiet and let the resident rest. Call this ONLY once they have made clear they'd like you to stop for now — either directly, or by accepting your gentle offer to go quiet. Before calling it, give a short warm sign-off (e.g. "Of course — I'll be right here whenever you want me. You rest now."). Do NOT call it just because they went briefly quiet; when you only SENSE they might be winding down, offer first and wait for their answer.
