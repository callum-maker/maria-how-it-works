Maria — How It Works

A visual map of what the companion can do, how information flows through it, and — importantly — who influences what information. Each tablet is one resident's private device; everything below lives on that device unless noted.

1What she can do

Capabilities, grouped. Each is either a live conversation skill, a proactive behaviour, or a staff tool.

💬 Natural conversation

Warm, short spoken replies via Claude, tuned for older adults; remembers the person across turns and days.

📖 Life-story reminiscence

Gently walks through life chapters, captures memories, comforts on grief, exits on distress.

🗓️ "What's on today"

Daily routine + schedule (upcoming only), then invites other activities.

🎛️ Tools

Weather, positive news, jokes, quotes, Wikipedia facts, song lyrics, poems, scripture, "on this day", web search.

📻 Music & radio

Plays internet radio / old songs; tap to stop.

📷 Family photos

Shows & talks about photos; weaves a new photo into the chat right away and asks if they'd like to see it.

💊 Medication reminders

Chime + on-screen card + gentle spoken line, at set times (time-critical, can interrupt).

🔁 Proactive routines

Scheduled trivia / news / music / check-ins / life-story chats — delivered only on a natural lull.

🛡️ Guardrails

No medical advice, crisis/emergency detection, avoid-topics honoured (incl. filtering the news) — with an optional "allow if the resident asks" override.

😴 Reads the room

"I'm resting / don't want to talk / good night" → goes quiet; offers rest on subtle cues.

📊 Staff insight

Wellbeing notes, conversation history, "what I've learned", life-story book (PDF).

🚀 Remote updates

New logic/prompts pushed over-the-air; auto-applies on idle. No cable.

7How much do residents actually use it? (real-world evidence)

The cost tiers below (1 / 2 / 5 hours a day) aren't guesses — they're anchored to published deployments and peer-reviewed studies of comparable AI voice companions in senior living and long-term care. These are different products, but they're the closest public evidence for how much older adults engage when given a proactive conversational companion.

Deployment / studySetting & scaleObserved usageHeadline outcome
ElliQ · Intuition Robotics + NY State Office for the Aging800+ older adults aging in place across New York State~30+ interactions/day, 6 days/week; first two weeks average ~120/day, then settle to roughly half. 75%+ of interactions tied to social / physical / mental well-being.95% of users reported reduced loneliness

🔎 What this means for our tiers

Engaged daily usage clusters around ~45–60 min/day (our 1-hour tier — the typical case). Motivated or lonely residents commonly reach 2+ hours (our 2-hour tier). Residents who find it genuinely companionable — or a shared device serving several residents — reach 3+ hours, which our 5-hour tier brackets as the heavy-engagement / multi-resident ceiling. So the cost math below maps onto observed behavior, not optimism.

How we actually get there

Each lever above, explained — why it saves money and what it takes to flip it on. Grouped from free-today to longer-term R&D.

💸 Free wins we can take today (no contracts, no migration)

Concise replies. TTS is billed per character and the brain per token — so a shorter reply cuts both bills at once, and speaks faster. Trimming a typical answer from ~600 to ~380 characters is a ~35% cut on the single most expensive line, achieved purely by tuning the system prompt and capping reply length. No code risk, no vendor change.

Cache the stock phrases. Greetings, medication reminders, daily check-ins, and little transitions ("let me think about that…") are spoken thousands of times with identical wording. We synthesize each once, save the audio, and replay the file — so those characters leave the TTS bill permanently and play back instantly (no synthesis wait).

Prompt caching on the brain (already on). Maria's big system prompt — persona, the resident's profile, safety rules — is the same every turn. Anthropic's prompt caching charges only ~10% to re-read that cached block, which is why the brain sits at ~$0.15/hr instead of $0.40+. It's live today; it just keeps paying off as conversations get longer.

📝 Contract & vendor moves (unlock at fleet scale)

Committed-use pricing. Azure Speech (and Anthropic) sell volume commitments: pledge a monthly floor of characters / audio-hours and list prices drop ~40–60%. This is a purchasing decision, not an engineering one — once a fleet of tablets generates predictable volume, we sign for the discount and every hour gets cheaper with zero code change.

Swap in a cheaper speech-to-text provider. Deepgram / AssemblyAI offer streaming recognition at roughly half Azure's per-hour rate with comparable accuracy. Because listening already sits behind one small interface (listen.ts), this is a contained provider swap — not a rewrite — and it stacks on top of the committed-use discount.

📱 Moving to the edge (the big one — longer-term R&D)

On-device speech-to-text → $0. Whisper or Vosk running on the tablet removes the ~$0.55/hr listening bill entirely — no audio ever hits a paid cloud. The gate is reliability: on-device endpointing and accuracy on older, accented, or quiet voices has to match what pushed us onto Azure in the first place. We prove that per-device before switching.

On-device text-to-speech → ~$0. Local neural voices (Piper, Kokoro, or platform voices) remove the ~$0.77/hr voice bill. This is the hardest tradeoff and the last we'd flip, because warmth is the product — on-device voices aren't yet as natural as Azure's Sonia, and we won't ship a colder Maria to save money. We move only when a local voice is genuinely good enough.

A small on-device brain for routine turns. A compact local model (Phi / Gemma / Llama-class) can handle simple chit-chat and navigation on-device, while cloud Claude is reserved for the moments that need real warmth or care judgment (grief, confusion, anything sensitive). This hybrid routing keeps the quality exactly where it matters while cutting cloud tokens on everything routine.

🚀 The moonshot in one line

A resident chatting with Maria 2 hours every single day costs about $90/month today → about $6/month at the ambitious target. That's ~10¢ per active hour, reached by putting the listening and the voice on the tablet itself and using the cloud brain sparingly. After showing it, describe it warmly using what you know about who and what is in it.
